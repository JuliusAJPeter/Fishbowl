Fishbowl conference rules

  • Only one person speaks at a time and there should be no side conversations.
  • Participants must use cues such as body language and eye contact to determine when it is appropriate to speak.
  • If two people start to speak at the same time, one must yield.
  • All participants must look at the person speaking and engage in active listening techniques (nodding, smiling, etc.).
  • Questions can and should be asked of other participants in the inner circle to motivate them to join in.
  • Disagree politely. Do not make it personal. You disagree with the idea, not the person.
  • State your claims and support them with evidence.
  • Respond to the comments of other participants by agreeing or disagreeing and by offering additional evidence.
  • If you are in the outer circle, record notes and quotations from the text(s) to support your claims.
  • The teacher does not participate in the discussion except to provide a new question or to terminate an irrelevant or inappropriate line of discussion. You should NOT look at the teacher while speaking but rather at the other participants.
